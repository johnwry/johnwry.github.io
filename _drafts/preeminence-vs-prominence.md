> He must increase, but I must decrease.
>
> John 3:30

Notice the order of that verse...It doesn't say I must decrease so that He might increase...rather focus is placed on Him increasing. *But* here could also be translated *and*. 



The preeminence of Christ in my life is a spiritual issue not a moral issue. It is not merely having a "Christian world-view" since any unbeliever can do that.  It is rather "*let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus*" and "*put on the mind of Christ*". Only a believer can have the mind of Christ. 

*The Christian life is not what I can do for God, but what Christ wants to* **be in me***. Therefore, when my purpose, goal, and aim in life is simply to know Christ-living a life of intimate, abiding fellowship in Him-then He will initiate and fulfill His good works through me (Eph 2:10). His good works are the overflow of my daily walk with Him-His life being lived through me-even without comprehending that which He is doing. And this will bring praise and honor and glory to Christ, the Preeminent One, "Christ in you, [which is] the hope of glory" (Col 1:27)* - Jerry Benjamin

### Lifestyle Christianity vs Christ Preeminent

In "lifestyle Christianity" Christ is the facilitator, and I am the initiator, the planner, the designer, and the strategist. Ultimately I am the one who has the final say. 

### Committed vs Presented

Committing or submitting (depending on how you define it)  has the appearance of spirituality while still maintaining control. 

To commit is to give my life to something or someone, while still retaining all rights and all authority to make the final decisions. I am still in control of my life. - Jerry Benjamin

When I present my life to someone, as long as I am presented to that someone, I am under their authority for I have entrusted myself to the care and control of that person. 

The imperative to the believer in Romans 6 verse 13 is to *present* (yield KJV) ourselves to God. When an army officer stands presented to his commanding officer, he has laid down his arms and stands at the disposition of the directive of the commander.  While he is presented to that commanding officer, he is under his direct authority.
